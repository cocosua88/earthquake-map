Tweets vs. Earthquakes About this project

    About this project

    It used to be that earthquakes were unpredictable natural disasters. But in modern Twitter age, people can learn about earthquakes before they feel it themselves. When we had 5.9 earthquake back in 2011 near Virginia, Twitter said that more than 400,000 earthquake-related tweets were sent within a minute of its occurrence, hitting a rate of about 5,500 tweets per second.1 Using USGS and Twitter API, this map compares the impact of earthquakes from USGS and twitter users activity on earthquake in real-time.
